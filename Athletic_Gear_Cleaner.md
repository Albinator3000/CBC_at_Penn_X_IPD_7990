# Athletic Gear Cleaner — Strategy: Augment

## Why Augment?

The Athletic Gear Cleaner is not primarily a technology product — it's a knowledge product. Anyone can sell a cleaning solution. The defensible position is owning the protocol: the structured, materials-science-backed framework that tells athletes exactly how to clean their specific gear, why certain approaches destroy certain materials, and what to do when the problem is more than surface-level.

That knowledge base is what makes the product worth paying for and worth trusting. Claude as a thinking partner is how you build it — faster and more thoroughly than any amount of manual research, and in a form you can keep updated as new gear materials hit the market.

---

## The Core Problem: Googling Gets It Wrong

The first result for "how to clean climbing shoes" will tell you to put them in a pillowcase in the washing machine. That destroys the rubber. The first result for "how to wash a football helmet" will miss the foam liner degradation issue. The first result for "how to clean a wetsuit" won't mention that hot water kills the neoprene bonding.

Your product's value is that it gets it right — and that it's specific enough to be genuinely useful. Augmentation is how you build that specificity at scale.

## Augmentation Strategies

### 1. Build the Core Knowledge Base

Use Claude to synthesize the underlying science for each gear category:

**By sport:** cycling, climbing, swimming, running, team sports (football, lacrosse, hockey), racquet sports, winter sports, water sports

**For each gear type, Claude generates:**
- Material composition (what it's made of and why that matters for cleaning)
- What cleaning agents are safe vs. damaging (and the chemistry behind why)
- Recommended cleaning method: hand wash vs. machine, water temperature, drying method
- Frequency guidelines: how often based on usage intensity
- Storage recommendations post-cleaning
- Warning signs of material breakdown that cleaning can't fix
- Common mistakes athletes make and the damage they cause

This knowledge base is the proprietary content moat. It's not available in this structured form anywhere on the internet.

### 2. Stress-Test Edge Cases

Use Claude to work through the hard cases before customers surface them:
- "Wetsuit with mold — is it salvageable or is the neoprene compromised?"
- "Carbon fiber cycling shoes with embedded electronics — water exposure risk?"
- "Football pads that smell but can't be fully submerged — what's the protocol?"
- "Goalie pads — composite foam, leather, synthetic outer — three different cleaning approaches in one piece of equipment"

Each edge case becomes a knowledge base entry. The product that handles the hard questions is the product that gets recommended.

### 3. Competitive and Regulatory Analysis

Use Claude to research:
- What do existing products (Gear Aid, Nikwax, Nathan Sports) cover well and where are their gaps?
- Are there any gear manufacturer warranties that are voided by specific cleaning methods? (This is a real concern — and knowing it is a trust-builder with customers)
- Are there any environmental regulations on cleaning solution ingredients relevant to your formulation or marketing?
- What do professional athletes and equipment managers use? (A different, higher-credibility knowledge source)

### 4. Protocol Template Design

Use Claude to design the structure of your cleaning protocol output so it's consistent, scannable, and genuinely useful:

```
GEAR: Road Cycling Shoes (synthetic upper, carbon sole, BOA closure)
PROBLEM: Odor + mud accumulation

WHAT YOU NEED: lukewarm water, soft brush, sports-specific detergent (not dish soap), towel
WHAT NOT TO USE: bleach, hot water, machine wash, direct sunlight drying

STEP-BY-STEP:
1. Remove insoles — wash separately by hand
2. Loosen mud with dry brush before any water contact
3. Wipe synthetic upper with damp cloth + minimal detergent
4. Carbon sole: wipe only — do not submerge
5. BOA closure: inspect cable while cleaning — replace if fraying
6. Air dry at room temperature — do not use heat

CARE SCHEDULE: After every wet ride; full clean monthly
EXPECTED OUTCOME: Odor eliminated in 1–2 treatments; mud removal immediate
```

Claude can generate this template structure and then populate it for every gear type in your catalog.

### 5. Content Freshness System

Use Claude to establish a process for keeping the knowledge base current:
- New materials (graphene composites, bio-based synthetics) entering the market — how do you update protocols?
- Customer feedback flagging protocols that didn't work — how do you investigate and revise?
- Manufacturer care guide changes — how do you monitor and incorporate?

Document this process. A knowledge base with a maintenance plan is an asset. One without is liability.
